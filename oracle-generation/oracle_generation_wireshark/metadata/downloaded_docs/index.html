Why Wireshark?

Free, Open Source, and Powerful

Comprehensive Network Analysis. Wireshark is a powerful, open-source network protocol analyzer that allows users to capture and interactively browse the traffic running on a computer network, providing deep inspection of hundreds of protocols.

Open Source and Free Forever. As an open-source tool, Wireshark is freely available for use and distribution, with its source code accessible under the GNU General Public License (GPL).

Supported by an Active Community. The Wireshark project is supported by a vibrant community of developers and users who contribute to its ongoing development and enhancement.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What platforms does Wireshark support?

    Wireshark runs on most computing platforms including Windows, macOS, Linux, and UNIX. It can also be built from source on other platforms. For more information, see the Download page.

  • Is Wireshark really free to use?

    Wireshark is "free software"; you can download it without paying any license fee. The version of Wireshark you download isn't a "demo" version, with limitations not present in a "full" version; it is the full version. The license under which Wireshark is issued is the GNU General Public License version 2. See the GNU GPL FAQ for some more information.


    Wireshark is maintained by the Wireshark Foundation , a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting the development and distribution of Wireshark. The Foundation is funded by donations from users and organizations that rely on Wireshark for their network analysis needs, consider supporting us by making a donation!

  • Where can I find tutorials and resources?

    Our Learn page is a great place to start. Our https://www.youtube.com/@WireSharkFest also has many tutorials and classes on various topics related to network analysis.
